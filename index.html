About Me

I am an Associate Professor in the School of Electrical, Computer, and Energy Engineering at Arizona State University, where I also hold a courtesy appointment as Graduate Faculty in the School of Computing and Augmented Intelligence. I am also an Amazon Scholar, working on machine learning and optimization problems.

My research lies at the intersection of machine learning, statistics, signal processing, and information theory. I develop algorithms that take a holistic approach to learning and data acquisition, with a focus on problems where data collection is expensive or constrained. My work often involves designing interactive learning algorithms that can efficiently leverage structure (like graphs, manifolds, and conservation laws) while actively deciding what data to collect next. Recent applications of my research include power system monitoring, pandemic prediction, meta-science, and neuroscience.

Before joining ASU, I was a Postdoctoral Fellow in Electrical and Computer Engineering at Rice University working with Rich Baraniuk. I completed my Ph.D. in the Machine Learning Department at Carnegie Mellon University under the guidance of Aarti Singh. I received my M.S. and Ph.D. in Electrical Engineering from the University of Wisconsin - Madison, where I was advised by Rob Nowak and Stark Draper.

Open Positions

I am always on the lookout for excellent Ph.D. students and postdocs who are interested in theoretical research in the areas of machine learning, high-dimensional statistics, information processing, and networks. Applications that have recently motivated my work include neuroscience, power system monitoring, sequential decision making, and pandemic prediction and prevention. If you think there is a match between our interests, please send me an email.

I would be particularly thrilled to receive applications from people in underrepresented groups in machine learning, such as women and BIPOC candidates.
